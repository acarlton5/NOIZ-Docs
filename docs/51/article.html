DCT Tools, Powers, and Limitations — Full Policy

The DCT (Dissonance Compliance Team) is responsible for platform-wide enforcement on NOIZ. This document explains the tools they use, what they’re allowed to do, and where their authority ends — so creators, viewers, and mods all understand the boundaries.

1. Why This Matters

The DCT holds significant authority across NOIZ. To ensure community trust, their role includes:

  • Strict accountability
  • Transparent tool usage
  • Defined access limits

Understanding these limits helps protect against misuse and builds confidence in our moderation systems.

2. Core Tools Available to DCT

Tool / PowerPurpose
🚫 Global BanRemove a user’s access to all streams and chat on NOIZ
🔇 ShadowmuteSilently hide a user’s messages platform-wide
❄️ ⚡︎dB FreezeTemporarily suspend monetization access
🎥 Stream LockoutEnd or restrict a live stream with high-risk or illegal content
🧾 Report FlaggingView and escalate reports from users, mods, and plugins
🔍 Mod Log AccessReview chat history and past moderation decisions
🛑 Exploit DetectionSystem alerts for impersonation, plugin abuse, or Decibel misuse
👁️ “Mod View” EntryTemporarily view flagged streams without alerting the chat

3. What DCT Can Do

✅ The DCT is authorized to:

  • Take enforcement actions after report review (ban, mute, freeze ⚡︎dB)
  • Observe live streams discreetly when flagged for violations
  • Review and audit moderation logs, chat, stream titles, and plugin activity
  • Pause or restrict publishing rights in the Marketplace (if abuse is detected)
  • Recommend de-listing from promotional spaces (e.g. Echostage, quests, boosts)
  • Escalate severe cases to Staff Admins or trigger full platform investigation

4. What DCT Cannot Do

❌ DCT members are explicitly prohibited from:

  • Unbanning users from individual creator channels
  • Assigning or removing Affiliate or Partner badges
  • Viewing or controlling Constellation app content outside of a flagged moderation case
  • Changing a stream’s title, category, or tags without approval
  • Overriding a Staff Admin ruling or monetization ban
  • Moderating cases where they have a personal relationship or bias

All conflicts of interest must be escalated and reassigned immediately.

5. Special Access: When and Why

Some tools (like Stream Lockout or invisible Mod View) are only permitted when:

  • A stream is actively endangering users or breaking the law
  • A report involves platform integrity (e.g. impersonation, CSAM, grooming)
  • A Staff Admin enables a compliance override session

These sessions are auto-logged and recorded for oversight. Logged metadata includes:

  • DCT member ID and time of access
  • Stream link and moderation action taken
  • IP address and session audit trail

6. Abuse Safeguards

To protect creators and viewers:

  • Every action taken by a DCT member is logged and reviewed
  • Suspected abuse triggers an instant Staff Admin audit
  • Confirmed abuse leads to:
    • Suspension of all tools
    • Internal review
    • Possible removal from the DCT

Anyone — streamer, mod, or viewer — can file a complaint about DCT misuse via:

→ Inbox → Report Staff Conduct → DCT Abuse

7. Transparency Reports

At NOIZ’s discretion, quarterly DCT Transparency Reports may be published. These show:

  • Number of reports reviewed
  • Types of actions taken
  • Appeal outcomes by category

These reports protect user privacy but help the community understand enforcement trends.

8. FAQ

Can the DCT see my wallet or cashout history?
→ No. Only if your account is flagged for ⚡︎dB abuse or fraud review.

What if I was flagged for impersonation unfairly?
→ You can file an appeal through the Account Actions panel.

Can the DCT watch my stream without joining chat?
→ Yes, but only if your stream is flagged for review.

Do DCT members get extra privileges as viewers?
→ No. They follow the same chat rules as any user outside of investigations.
