Moderator Permissions, Tools & Training

Channel moderators are a critical part of NOIZ's community safety system. They help uphold each creator's tone while enforcing core platform rules.

What Channel Moderators Are

Channel moderators (mods) are creator-assigned users who help:

  • Keep chat clean, inclusive, and on-topic
  • Enforce community rules and tagging expectations
  • Flag serious violations to DCT (platform moderation)

Mods are not NOIZ staff, but their behavior reflects the creator's brand. All mod actions are visible in chat history and mod logs.

Permissions Mods Have

PermissionDescription
/timeout @userTemporarily disables chat for that user (duration settable)
/ban @userRemoves user from chat until unbanned manually
/purge @userDeletes a user’s recent messages
Message deletionRemoves individual messages via hover or tap
Flag for DCT reviewEscalates content for platform-level investigation
Highlight messagesMarks messages for streamer attention
Mod dashboard accessEnables tools like viewer notes, mod-only chat, slow mode

Mods do not have access to:

  • ⚡︎dB balances
  • Creator dashboard tools
  • Channel editor features (unless assigned both roles)

Tools Available to Moderators

Mods can use the Mod Panel to:

  • Manage chat filters and AutoMod (if permitted by the creator)
  • Enable slow mode or keyword-based delay
  • Coordinate in mod-only chat
  • Review mod logs and timeouts
  • Request additions to the blocked word list

All tools are scoped to the stream they were assigned in.

Mod Expectations

Good moderators:

  • Are attentive and calm during broadcasts
  • Communicate warnings clearly, without escalating tension
  • Use timeouts as a first step (when appropriate)
  • Avoid abuse of power or retaliatory actions
  • Respect viewer boundaries and DCT policy limits

Streamers are expected to:

  • Assign mods they trust
  • Provide clear guidelines and escalation examples
  • Revoke mod access if abuse is reported or confirmed

Escalation Protocol

If a moderator encounters:

  • Hate speech
  • Child endangerment
  • Threats or doxxing
  • Suspicious links, scams, or botting

They must use the Flag for DCT Review tool, which:

  • Captures the message or user
  • Sends a snapshot to platform enforcement
  • Notifies the stream owner (if enabled)

Critical or legal-risk cases are escalated to Staff Admin automatically.

Mod Training Checklist (Streamer Resource)

Creators should onboard new moderators with:

  • A mod-only Discord or overlay panel
  • A pinned message listing:
    • Custom channel rules
    • Banned phrases
    • Shortcut commands and timing expectations
  • A training drill or mock stream covering:
    • Harassment response
    • Spam handling
    • Proper use of highlight and flagging tools

Moderator Conduct Enforcement

Mods who:

  • Harass users
  • Leak private information
  • Weaponize their status

May face:

  • Immediate mod removal (by the creator)
  • Platform-wide tool suspension
  • Review by DCT or Staff Admin in extreme cases

Repeat violations can affect eligibility for mod roles platform-wide.

FAQ

Can I give a mod access to channel editor tools?
Yes, but only by assigning them that role separately.

Can mods unban users?
Yes, unless the creator disables this permission.

What if a mod is falsely reported?
Reports are reviewed case-by-case; mods are notified only if relevant.

Can mods earn ⚡︎dB or Quest rewards?
Mods earn support like any other viewer unless otherwise restricted by the stream.

Are mod actions visible to other viewers?
Some are (e.g., timeouts), but mod-only notes and flags are private.
