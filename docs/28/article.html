This guide explains how account access, session control, and token security work on NOIZ — for both creators and viewers.

1. NOIZ Accounts Use Token-Based Access

Unlike traditional platforms, NOIZ accounts do not rely on usernames or passwords. Instead, access is secured using the Constellation handshake system, which replaces insecure login credentials with time-limited, device-authenticated tokens.

Your account is protected by:

  • A 6-digit Constellation handshake code (single-use)
  • Authenticated device links (via the Constellation mobile app)
  • Verified connection sessions (you can revoke access at any time)
  • 2FA options inside your Constellation payout settings (for eligible users)

Important: Never share your handshake code or QR token on stream or with others. These can be used to impersonate your account temporarily.

3. For Creators: Stream-Specific Security

Creators have additional responsibilities around session and stream control. To stay protected:

  • Rotate your stream key if switching software or devices
  • Never show your Constellation login codes or QR tokens on stream
  • Restrict access to stream controls (limit to moderators or channel editors only)
  • Audit regularly:
    • Channel Editors
    • Approved Marketplace plugins
    • Connected overlays or automation tools

Hosting carries responsibility. If your channel is used to display another stream, you may still be held accountable for violations. Choose Editors carefully.

4. For Viewers: Privacy & Safety Settings

All viewers have access to platform-wide privacy tools:

  • Block users or hide chat content
  • Disable leaderboard visibility (e.g., hide your gifting totals)
  • Use anonymous mode when sending Decibels
  • Opt out of Quest profile tracking from Settings → Privacy & Safety

These controls give viewers full command over how they participate and appear on NOIZ.

5. Constellation Device Safety

Treat Constellation like your identity key:

  • Install it only on your personal device
  • If lost or compromised:
    • Log in on a new device
    • Revoke access to the old one immediately via Active Connections
    • Contact NOIZ Support to request a session lock or temporary freeze
  • Keep the Constellation app updated — new security updates are pushed regularly

6. Reporting Suspicious Activity

Use NOIZ’s built-in reporting system to flag:

  • Unauthorized ⚡︎dB transfers
  • Suspicious account access
  • Potential fraud in quests or Marketplace assets

Your report is routed to:

  • The creator (if the issue is channel-specific)
  • DCT (Dissonance Compliance Team) for platform moderation
  • Staff Admins for legal or financial escalations

7. NOIZ's Role in Account Protection

What we do:

  • Monitor for botting, phishing, and self-support loops
  • Automatically flag suspicious gifting or boost patterns
  • Notify you of new device logins or session connections

What we don’t:

  • Store your private Constellation device information
  • Ask for seed phrases, passwords, or private keys
  • Share personal data with advertisers or third-party ad tech

8. FAQ

Q: What if I accidentally shared a handshake code?
A: Revoke the session via Constellation immediately and reauthenticate on a secure device.

Q: Can someone take my account by knowing my channel name?
A: No. Without your verified Constellation token and code, no login can occur.

Q: How do I reset my NOIZ access if my phone is lost?
A: Log into Constellation on another device and revoke the old session. If needed, contact Support to request a session reset.

Q: Does NOIZ offer recovery for stolen ⚡︎dB?
A: If theft is confirmed through fraud or compromise, ⚡︎dB may be restored at NOIZ’s discretion. Abuse or negligence may void eligibility.
