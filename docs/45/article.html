1. What Counts as Trolling

On NOIZ, trolling includes:

  • Repetitive spamming or baiting
  • Disruptive messages designed to throw off the stream
  • Passive-aggressive or sarcastic escalation
  • Raiding with hostile or combative viewers
  • Provoking reactions to clip or repost out of context

Trolling = pattern-based disruption. Even if messages are technically ‚Äúclean,‚Äù intent and effect matter.

Mods should:

  • Ban ringleader accounts quickly
  • Use /purge or message deletion tools to wipe spam
  • Report suspected coordination using Flag for DCT Review

5. Reaction Management

Trolls seek attention ‚Äî emotional or performative responses help them.

Instead, streamers can:

  • Use Scene Swap (change visuals or mute chat)
  • Trigger Momentai Mode for auto-toning tools (chat slowdown, mood filters)
  • Let mods handle the issue without calling it out on-stream

The best reaction is often no reaction.

6. Aftercare: Resetting the Tone

Post-troll recovery tips:

  • Thank mods and supportive viewers
  • Promote positive engagement to refill chat with normal energy
  • Review your block list and flag logs
  • Adjust filters or safety settings as needed

Report serious incidents if:

  • Hate speech or targeted harassment was used
  • Trolls targeted a minor, creator identity, or mental health
  • The event included plugin exploits or marketplace spam
