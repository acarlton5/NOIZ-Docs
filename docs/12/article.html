What Is a Channel Editor on NOIZ?

A Channel Editor is a trusted user role that creators can assign to help manage and maintain their streams, quests, and visual elements. Editors act as platform-level collaborators and have access to critical stream tools.

Unlike Moderators, who are focused on chat behavior and enforcement, Editors directly interact with channel-level systems, stream metadata, and can even start or stop your stream.

Editors should be selected with care — they can impact your channel’s public-facing experience and represent you in key ways.

How to Assign an Editor

To add someone as an Editor:

  1. Go to your Creator Dashboard
  2. Navigate to: Settings → Team Access
  3. Add the user via:
    • Their Constellation-linked user ID, or
    • Their verified NOIZ channel name
  4. The invited Editor will receive a confirmation request. Access is not granted until accepted.
  5. You may remove or revoke Editor status at any time from this same panel.

Editors do not automatically become Moderators. These are distinct roles and must be assigned separately if both permissions are desired.

Editor Access and Trust

Editors represent your channel in various contexts.

  • Host other streams from your account
  • Adjust metadata that appears in search or category directories
  • Influence your presence on Echostage and stream discoverability

Because of these abilities, Editors are accountability-linked to the creator. Choose team members you trust completely.

What's the Difference Between a Mod and an Editor?

While both are supportive roles, their function and risk levels are very different.

RolePrimary FocusRepresents the Creator?Platform Risk Level
Moderator (Mod)Chat moderation, rule enforcement, emote controlsNo – moderates chat behavior, not channel settingsLower risk
Channel EditorQuest and overlay management, stream metadata, hostingYes – changes affect the creator’s channel directlyHigher risk

Key Difference:
Mods focus on what happens in the chat. Editors manage what shows up on your page.

Important Note on Hosting:
Editors can host another creator on your behalf. If that hosted stream violates NOIZ policies (e.g., mature content mislabeling), you may be held accountable under the Hosting Policy — even if you were not live at the time.

Best Practices for Managing Editors

  • Regularly review your Editor team for relevance and trust
  • Revoke access immediately if trust is compromised
  • Avoid assigning Editor status unless you’ve worked with the person before
  • Clearly define expectations: editing tools vs chat behavior
