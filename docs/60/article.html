Whether you’re sharing a developer reveal or letting a guest run your channel, this guide covers what’s allowed — and what you’re accountable for.

1. What Is Co-streaming on NOIZ?

Co-streaming = Multiple streamers covering the same event on their own channels — often in sync, with commentary, visuals, or overlays.

Common use cases:

  • Game reveals and developer showcases
  • Sponsored Quests with co-op or sync conditions
  • Tournament reactions or shared gameplay
  • Partner-approved media (trailers, demos, livestreams)

❗ Co-streaming is not the same as rebroadcasting someone else’s stream. There must be commentary, transformation, or synchronized participation.

2. What Is a Stream Takeover?

A Stream Takeover = Allowing another verified user to go live directly on your channel — using your chat, overlays, and layout.

Great for:

  • Guest creators
  • Charitable or mod-hosted events
  • Developer Q&A
  • Artist spotlights

Takeovers require explicit access, either through:

  • Your dashboard (one-time keys or permissions)
  • A plugin managing stream access with delegated credentials

3. What’s Allowed in Co-streaming

✅ You can:

  • React to licensed or authorized game/event content
  • Use the Multi-View plugin to merge audio or split screens
  • Run synchronized quests or challenges with another streamer
  • Co-stream NOIZ-sponsored broadcasts (if listed as eligible)
  • Share Discord visuals (if you have the rights)

Example layouts:

  • Dual-view with your stream + another
  • Side-by-side commentary
  • Shared gameplay with distinct overlays

4. What’s Not Allowed

🚫 You may not:

  • Restream another user’s full stream without collaboration
  • Claim someone else’s gameplay or footage as your own
  • Re-air copyrighted media without commentary/transformation
  • Give channel access to banned or unverified users
  • Let guests run takeovers that violate your channel’s maturity rating or rules

5. Stream Takeover Rules

If you let someone else stream on your channel:

🛑 You are responsible for everything that happens.

Required:

  • Tag the stream correctly (e.g., Mature, 18+, etc.)
  • Be available to revoke access instantly if needed
  • Only grant access via dashboard or verified plugins

Go to:

→ Dashboard → Channel Access → Guest Streamer

From there, you can:

  • Issue time-limited stream keys
  • Restrict overlays, bitrate, resolution
  • Schedule takeovers and auto-revoke
  • Monitor usage logs in real time

6. Visual Layouts & Plugin Support

NOIZ supports:

  • Split-screen for co-op or esports
  • PiP overlays for commentary or guests
  • Audio channel control (volume separation, mute priority)
  • Multi-Quest plugins (for synchronized events)

These tools are optional — but make co-streaming smoother and safer.

7. Monetization Rules for Co-streams

⚡︎dB and boosts always go to the channel being streamed on.

  • Guests do not earn from your viewers unless:
    • The stream is mirrored on their own channel
    • They are also part of the registered Quest campaign

📌 If part of a sponsored Quest, follow the campaign’s co-streaming rules:

  • NOIZ may require exclusive focus
  • Dual streams may not be eligible for reward milestones unless approved

8. FAQ

Can I co-stream Twitch, Kick, or YouTube content? → Only if you have rights or it’s public domain (e.g. game trailers, esports broadcasts with co-stream rights)

Can my guest earn ⚡︎dB while using my stream? → No — your channel receives all earnings unless they co-broadcast on their own profile

Can I limit plugin use or overlays during a takeover? → Yes — guest settings let you lock overlays, plugins, and stream inputs

What happens if my guest breaks the rules? → Your channel is liable. Violations during takeovers are treated as if you streamed them directly
