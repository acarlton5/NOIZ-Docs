NOIZ is engineered for live-first content. Latency, quality, and interactivity are tuned for creators — not delayed by ad servers or legacy formats.

1. What Viewers Experience on NOIZ

NOIZ is built for real-time interactivity, meaning:

  • ⚡ Latency is typically under 500ms
  • 📍 Viewers are auto-routed through the nearest optimal relay
  • 🎬 There’s no 10-second delay like on HLS platforms

This is possible because NOIZ uses:

  • ITG (Instant Transmission Grid) — a continuous packet protocol
  • Adaptive relays — smart route switching based on viewer/device
  • Minimal handshake time — no startup chunk buffering

2. What Streamers Control

Streamers choose their own:

  • 🎚️ Bitrate and resolution
  • 🧪 Encoder settings (hardware or software)
  • 🎥 Streaming tools (NOIZ Studio, OBS, custom RTMP)

✅ NOIZ never injects forced delay into streams.

These settings are applied through:

  • NOIZ Studio → auto-config wizard
  • OBS or 3rd-party → manual RTMP input

3. Recommended Bitrate Settings

Resolution FPS Recommended Bitrate Notes
720p 30fps 2,000–3,500 kbps Minimum for reliable streams
720p 60fps 3,000–4,500 kbps Smooth motion for games or action
1080p 30fps 4,000–6,000 kbps Strong fidelity for general streams
1080p 60fps 6,000–9,000 kbps High performance streamers preferred
1440p/4K 30+fps 9,000–14,000 kbps (beta) Limited relay support during testing

🧠 Use hardware encoders (NVENC, AMD VCE, Apple VT) whenever possible for best performance.

4. Transcoding Availability

Transcoding lets viewers:

  • Switch between quality levels (high/medium/low)
  • Avoid stalling on slower networks
  • Match stream quality to mobile or older hardware

📡 Transcoding is enabled automatically when available:

Channel Tier Transcoding?
Partner ✅ Always available
Affiliate 🟡 Priority, but limited
Everyone Else 🔄 Based on capacity

No extra setup required — NOIZ handles it in the background.

5. Viewer Device Optimization

NOIZ auto-detects and adjusts for:

  • 📱 Mobile playback vs desktop
  • 🌐 Low-bandwidth or high-packet-loss connections
  • 🖥️ 60fps+ displays and high refresh rates

You don’t need to stream multiple variants — NOIZ relays + transcoding handle viewer-side optimization in real time.

6. How to Improve Your Latency

🛠 Tips for lowest stream delay:

  • ✅ Use NOIZ Studio (optimized ingest and relay sync)
  • 🛰️ Host your own relay node (Pathweaver program)
  • 🔓 Avoid VPNs or routers that block UDP traffic
  • ⚙️ Enable Low Latency Mode in OBS or your encoder

Even without a custom relay, most creators see ~300–500ms glass-to-glass latency.

7. FAQ

What if my viewers are buffering a lot?
→ Try lowering bitrate or checking your relay route in the Creator Dashboard.

Can I add a delay for tournaments or speedruns?
→ Yes — set manual delay in OBS or NOIZ Studio’s advanced settings.

Is transcoding available to small/new channels?
→ Yes, but only as capacity allows. Affiliates get priority during peak hours.

Can I stream in 4K?
→ You can — but relay and transcoding support is currently in limited beta. Watch for updates.
