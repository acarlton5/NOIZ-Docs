This document explains how NOIZ protects creators from DDoS attacks, conceals origin IP addresses, and prevents network-based targeting. Our infrastructure is built to isolate, obfuscate, and secure all broadcast activity — by default.

1. What Is a DDoS Attack?

A Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS) attack occurs when a malicious actor floods a specific IP address with excessive traffic. This can:

  • Interrupt your internet connection
  • Cause your stream to drop or lag
  • Force your device or router offline

Streamers on peer-to-peer services or platforms that expose their IP (e.g., Discord, certain RTMP tools) are highly vulnerable to these attacks.

⚠️ NOIZ was architected from day one to prevent these attacks at the network layer — no need for third-party VPNs or masking tools.

2. How NOIZ Protects Your IP

Your real IP address is never exposed when using NOIZ. This applies across the entire platform, including:

  • Viewer access
  • Relay transmission (Pathweavers)
  • Plugin or overlay integrations

All stream traffic is routed through:

  • Secure NOIZ ingress gateways
  • Sanitized and sandboxed relay nodes
  • Obfuscated outbound stream endpoints

Even if a malicious actor inspects your stream path, they’ll only encounter relay IPs — never your device or home network.

3. Relay Architecture and DDoS Safety

NOIZ uses a hybrid infrastructure composed of platform-operated gateways and Pathweavers (community-hosted relay nodes). The architecture is built to de-risk IP exposure and isolate bad actors.

Relay properties:

  • Never pull data directly from a creator’s IP
  • Are assigned dynamically by region and trust score
  • Are monitored in real-time for:
    • Packet anomalies
    • Spoof attempts
    • Identity leaks or unauthorized logging

Relay operators cannot see your IP or inspect raw socket traffic.

4. Extra Measures for Creators

You do not need to configure a VPN or third-party proxy to stay safe on NOIZ.

✅ NOIZ Studio, our OBS fork, auto-obfuscates stream handshake metadata

✅ Stream handshake routing is encrypted and tokenized

When setting your route, you can choose:

  • Auto-secure (recommended for best balance of speed and protection)
  • Manual safe relay (for advanced users or regional control)

5. How Plugins & Overlays Stay Safe

All tools and overlays on NOIZ are sandboxed under strict platform policies.

No plugin can:

  • Access browser-level or device IPs
  • Interact with raw socket traffic
  • Circumvent permission-based scopes

Creators and developers cannot upload tools to the Marketplace that gather external IP data or establish P2P behavior. These restrictions are built into the platform runtime and enforced at upload.

6. What to Do If You Suspect a DDoS

First, rule out normal connection issues. Ask yourself:

  • Is your internet unstable on all platforms?
  • Are you losing connection to services outside of NOIZ?

If you suspect a targeted attack:

  1. End your stream immediately
  2. Reboot your modem/router
  3. Request a new IP address from your ISP (if possible)
  4. File a report under:
    Settings → Report → “Targeted Network Attack”

NOIZ will temporarily harden your stream routing.

7. How NOIZ Responds

If you submit a report or DDoS is detected through logs:

  • Your relay path may be forcibly rerouted through shielded NOIZ gateways
  • All connection logs and packet routes are reviewed
  • Attackers are:
    • Flagged internally
    • Device-banned via ingress filtering
    • Investigated for coordinated abuse

If the attack occurred off-platform (e.g., Discord voice, Twitch stream), NOIZ may recommend that you contact your ISP or local cybersecurity authorities.

8. FAQ

Do I need a VPN to stream on NOIZ?
No. VPNs are not required — NOIZ handles IP masking automatically.
Can someone find my IP if they gift me Decibels or interact in chat?
Absolutely not. No support action on NOIZ ever reveals a creator’s IP.
Are relay operators able to trace my real location?
No. Relays never receive your origin IP. All stream data is forwarded from a NOIZ-operated ingress node.
What if I host a relay — can I get attacked instead?
Hosting a relay is voluntary and includes traffic shaping protections. Relay operators are sandboxed from DDoS targeting and abuse vectors, and relay IPs are regularly cycled.
