Why Disclosure Matters on NOIZ

Transparency is essential to trust — especially in a creator-first ecosystem like NOIZ.

Whether you're receiving ⚡︎dB, free items, visibility boosts, or brand partnership perks, you must disclose any form of sponsored content. This protects you and your viewers, and keeps NOIZ compliant with:

  • U.S. Federal Trade Commission (FTC) guidelines
  • International disclosure laws and advertising codes
  • NOIZ platform integrity policies

Disclosure is about clarity, not punishment. Viewers deserve to know when what they're watching has been paid for or promoted.

What Counts as a Paid Promotion

A paid promotion is any content or interaction that is supported, funded, or rewarded by another entity — including NOIZ.

You must disclose if you receive:

  • ⚡︎dB (Charged Decibels) from a brand, publisher, or sponsor
  • Free products tied to expected coverage or exposure
  • Paid or sponsored Quests
  • Bonuses, boosts, or visibility based on content performance

Common examples include:

  • Brand-sponsored Quests or campaigns
  • Paid plugin showcases or overlay usage
  • Product trials with the expectation of a shoutout
  • Partnered co-streams, interviews, or event coverage

If you’re receiving anything of value — whether digital or physical — and there’s an expectation to feature it, it qualifies as a paid promotion.

How to Disclose Properly

Disclosures must be:

  • Clear and unambiguous
  • Visible or audible at the start of a stream, quest, or segment
  • Repeated as needed, especially for long broadcasts or repeated sponsored segments

Acceptable formats:

  • Sponsored tag: apply the platform's [Sponsored] tag to your stream or quest
  • Visual overlay: watermark, banner, or “Sponsored by” tag
  • Verbal: “This segment is sponsored by...” at the beginning
  • Quest description: must clearly identify the sponsor

Platform tools you should use:

  • Sponsored Quest tag (when launching quests with brand backing)
  • Disclosure overlay widget (in development)

What NOT to Do

Avoid vague or misleading references. Specifically, do not:

  • Use phrases like “special thanks to...” without stating it’s paid
  • Frame sponsored content as spontaneous or organic if it was pre-arranged
  • Let the sponsor write viewer-facing content in your voice
  • Tie viewer support to deceptive incentives like:

    “Tip 1,000 dB and I guarantee a win”
    “Support this and I’ll reveal a secret” (if not legitimate)

Examples of Safe Disclosures

Scenario Safe Disclosure Example
Sponsored Quest from a game studio “This quest is sponsored by [Studio Name]!”
Paid plugin showcase “I was sponsored to try this overlay tool.”
Free product sent for testing “I got this headset for free from [Brand]!”
Brand-funded co-stream “Today’s stream is in partnership with [Brand]!”

Consequences of Non-Disclosure

Failure to disclose sponsored content is a violation of both:

  • NOIZ Terms of Use
  • National advertising laws

If you fail to disclose:

  • The content may be removed
  • Associated ⚡︎dB may be revoked
  • Your monetization tools may be paused or revoked
  • You may face moderation strikes or platform suspension
  • You may be subject to legal enforcement from governing bodies (e.g., FTC)

Disclosure in Quests, Overlays, and Marketplace

Sponsored Quests:

  • Must include the name of the sponsor or campaign in the Quest title or description

Marketplace:

  • Packs, plugins, or overlays funded or promoted by brands must include a disclosure in:
    • The listing title (e.g., [Sponsored Pack])
    • The product description
    • Any preview or demo content

Overlay Use:

  • If your overlays or visual assets are sponsored, use:
    • A sponsor watermark
    • A “Supported by [Brand]” banner or tag

FAQ

Do I need to disclose if I support a brand voluntarily?
No — only if the brand compensated you in ⚡︎dB, items, or visibility, or if there’s an expectation of coverage.
What if I wasn’t paid in money, just Decibels?
You still must disclose. ⚡︎dB is a virtual token of value on NOIZ and counts as compensation.
Does viewer gifting or boosting count as promotion?
Not unless they were part of a pre-arranged sponsorship or incentive program.
Can I promote sponsors on social media or off-platform without disclosing?
Disclosure laws apply across platforms. Even tweets or Instagram stories must be tagged if promotional.
