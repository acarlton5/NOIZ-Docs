How to share your stream across the web — safely and effectively

NOIZ lets you embed your stream on websites, games, and event pages — but it comes with important visibility, branding, and safety rules.

1. What Is Stream Embedding?

Embedding = placing your NOIZ stream and/or chat into an external site or app using HTML.

Examples:

  • Creator websites
  • Game launcher pages
  • Esports hubs or studio showcases
  • React or Next.js-based partner pages

Embeds include:

  • 📺 Live player
  • 💬 Optional chat
  • ⚡︎ Support tools (tips, follows, emotes, etc.)

2. How to Embed a NOIZ Stream

From your dashboard:

  1. Go to Channel Settings → Share & Embed
  2. Choose:
    • Stream Only (video player only)
    • Stream + Chat (full experience)
  3. Copy the HTML iframe code
  4. Paste it into:
    • Your website
    • CMS tools (e.g., WordPress, Wix)
    • Custom frontend apps (e.g., React components)

Embeds work out of the box and update dynamically as your stream goes live.

3. Embed Permissions & Visibility

✅ You control where your stream is allowed to embed.

From Channel Settings → Advanced Privacy, you can:

  • Restrict allowed domains (e.g., only mygame.com)
  • Hide chat by default
  • Force maturity warnings on embed
  • Disable embedding entirely

This is especially useful for creators who only want their content visible inside specific platforms or apps.

4. When Embedding Is Recommended

✅ Good use cases:

  • Promotional microsites
  • Streamer hubs or collab pages
  • Studio or publisher partner events
  • Game launcher integrations

🛑 Not allowed:

  • Piracy or hate speech sites
  • Without the streamer’s consent (if embedding someone else)
  • Streams relying on plugins that break outside the NOIZ interface

5. Interactivity & Plugin Compatibility

NOIZ embeds support many plugin features:

✅ Still work:

  • Vibe animations
  • Emotes
  • Reactions and view-based milestones

⚠️ Limited or disabled:

  • Chat commands (if chat is disabled)
  • Login-dependent tools (e.g. Quest buttons, ⚡︎ tipping)
  • Interactive overlays that require NOIZ session tokens

💡 Tip: Test your stream in an embedded view before launch — some layouts or plugins may need adjustment.

6. Branding & Attribution Rules

All embedded NOIZ streams must include:

  • NOIZ logo/watermark
  • Link back to the original channel (noiz.gg/yourname)
  • Title and tags visible or accessible

🚫 Do not:

  • Remove or obscure platform UI
  • Add 3rd-party ad overlays or popups
  • Use embeds to mirror or re-stream NOIZ content on competitive platforms

7. FAQ

Can I autoplay embedded streams?
→ Yes, but browsers may block autoplay with audio by default.

Can I hide chat?
→ Yes — or switch it to view-only mode.

Do embed viewers count for ⚡︎dB and Quest milestones?
→ Yes — as long as they are real, engaged viewers (not bots or background tabs).

Can I embed someone else’s stream?
→ Yes — if their channel allows it and you do not alter the experience.
