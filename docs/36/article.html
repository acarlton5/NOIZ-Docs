1. What Counts as Mature Content

Mature content refers to any stream element that may not be appropriate for all audiences, including:

  • Profanity, vulgar language, or graphic jokes
  • Explicit or heavy thematic content (e.g. sex, trauma, violence, gore)
  • Depictions or discussion of alcohol, drugs, or tobacco
  • Mature-rated games with blood, horror, or suggestive content
  • Innuendo or lewd humor (without being sexually explicit)

Mature does not mean adult entertainment. Content should still serve a creative, narrative, or expressive purpose — not exist solely for shock or sexual provocation.

2. What’s Allowed with a Mature Label

You may stream the following, if properly labeled:

  • Games rated Mature or 18+, including horror, FPS, or survival titles
  • IRL conversations involving adult themes or lived experience (e.g., relationships, nightlife, trauma)
  • Casual use of swearing, crude jokes, or adult-oriented humor
  • References to drinking, smoking, or partying in a storytelling context

If your stream includes any of the above, you must use:

  • The "Mature" tag
  • A stream rating of M or 18+
  • Let NOIZ’s auto age-gating apply

Tags and ratings are not optional — they’re required for both viewer safety and legal compliance.

3. What Is Not Allowed — Even With a Mature Tag

The following are strictly prohibited, regardless of maturity rating or stream context:

  • Pornographic content (live, pre-recorded, or animated)
  • Nudity, sexual activity, or simulation of sexual acts
  • Depictions of minors in any sexual or suggestive context (zero tolerance)
  • Real-world violence (e.g., fights, injury stunts, accidents)
  • Promotion of illegal behavior (e.g., drug use on camera, reckless dares)
  • Streams whose primary focus is sexual content or titillation

NOIZ is not an adult content platform. Mature tags are for thematic or expressive context — not a license to break ToS.

5. Creator Responsibilities

As a creator, you are responsible for:

  • Applying the correct stream rating and mature tags before going live
  • Updating the tags if your stream changes tone or content mid-session
  • Using overlays or chat descriptions to clarify what viewers can expect
  • Moderating your chat to prevent viewer behavior from becoming inappropriate or harassing

Mature doesn’t mean “anything goes.” Respectful boundaries still apply — for both the content and the community around it.

6. Viewer Protections

Mature content is automatically:

  • Age-gated using Constellation-linked birthdate
  • Hidden from underage viewers and searchable filters (based on settings)
  • Flagged with a “Mature Content” warning before the stream can be entered

Viewers can also:

  • Disable all mature stream visibility in their Settings
  • Report miscategorized streams that appear to bypass content policies

If a stream isn’t properly tagged, you can help keep the platform safe by reporting it.

7. Violations and Enforcement

Failing to follow mature content rules can lead to:

  • Content flagging and visibility restrictions
  • Warnings or temporary suspension for misleading tags or misratings
  • Immediate ban and Staff Admin escalation for sexual exploitation, predatory behavior, or repeat violations

NOIZ reserves the right to:

  • Mute or remove streams retroactively
  • Flag channels for maturity non-compliance
  • Enforce stricter controls for repeat offenders

8. FAQ

Can I stream an M-rated game without a mature tag?
No — any game rated M or containing frequent violence or adult content must be labeled.

Is cosplay or themed attire allowed on a mature stream?
Yes, if it complies with the Clothing & Expression Policy and is not sexually explicit.

What if my chat is mature but the game isn’t?
You still need to tag your stream if the discussion or tone is adult in nature.

How do I get my stream reclassified if it was flagged incorrectly?
Use the appeal tool under Creator Dashboard → Content Flag → Request Review.
