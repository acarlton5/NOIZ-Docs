1. Why Username Rules Exist

Usernames are the most visible part of a user's identity on NOIZ — across chat, stream overlays, dashboards, and search results.

This policy exists to:

  • Prevent harassment, impersonation, or abuse
  • Ensure safety for marginalized users and minors
  • Protect creators, staff, and brands from confusion or fraud
  • Maintain a consistent, welcoming platform environment

2. General Username Rules

A username must not:

  • Contain hate speech, slurs, or coded extremist language
  • Be sexually explicit, graphically suggestive, or fetishistic
  • Use excessive profanity or adult slang
  • Impersonate another creator, NOIZ staff, or public figure
  • Mimic brand names, franchises, or game studios unless part of a verified account

Usernames are visible in chat, on your channel, and in shared events — they must be safe for all users, including minors.

✅ Allowed Examples (playful or edgy, but not in violation):

  • SilentShadow
  • N00bSlayer88
  • QuestDad420 (mild humor, not explicit)

❌ Not Allowed Examples (violate clarity, safety, or impersonation rules):

  • nazi_power (hate speech)
  • cum4cash (graphically sexual)
  • NOIZ_Admin (staff-only naming convention)
  • BlizzardOfficial (brand impersonation)

3. Username Moderation Triggers

Usernames may be flagged by:

  • Community reports
  • Automated filters
  • Platform audits

NOIZ moderation will review based on:

  • Contextual use (e.g., "daddy" alone vs. “sexydaddyass69”)
  • Cultural intent vs. known slur or abuse patterns
  • History of prior naming or chat violations

False positives are possible — decisions include nuance, not just keyword checks.

4. Blocked Word List (Auto-Filtered)

NOIZ maintains an automated filter of restricted terms, including:

  • Known slurs, hate group references, extremist symbols
  • Sexually explicit phrases or anatomical terms
  • Platform-reserved prefixes, including:
    • NOIZ_ (staff-only)
    • DCT, Pathweaver, Partner+ (badge-locked roles)
  • Brand impersonation attempts, e.g.:
    • riotgames_support, steam_moderator, nintendoquest
  • Domain-like names, e.g.:
    • noizgiveaways.com, freebits.org

If a blocked name is bypassed and flagged after creation, users will be notified and required to select a new name.

5. Impersonation & Brand Misuse

NOIZ reserves the right to rename, restrict, or reassign usernames that:

  • Mimic verified streamers, Staff, or DCT members
  • Resemble partnered brand or game names
  • Are reserved for platform campaigns or sponsorships
  • Appear to be fake support accounts or moderation roles

Impersonation is a serious violation and may result in monetization hold, especially if used to mislead or defraud.

6. Name Change Policy

  • Users may change their username once every 90 days (free)
  • Additional name changes may be delayed or locked without platform approval

If a username violates guidelines, NOIZ may:

  • Issue a forced rename request
  • Apply a temporary or permanent suspension if not resolved
  • Place a ⚡︎dB hold if the name was used to earn support while impersonating others

7. FAQ

Q: Can I use “admin” or “mod” in my name?
A: No — these are reserved for staff or trusted roles and will trigger moderation flags.

Q: What if my username is mature but my stream is tagged accordingly?
A: Explicit usernames are never allowed, even with Mature stream tags. Channel maturity applies to content, not profile visibility.

Q: What if someone is squatting a name I want?
A: NOIZ does not allow name sniping or squatting for resale. You may submit a name release request if the account is inactive and not verified.

Q: Can NOIZ reserve a name for a future project or campaign?
A: Yes. The platform may lock usernames for verified partners, games, or sponsor initiatives to prevent abuse or misrepresentation.
