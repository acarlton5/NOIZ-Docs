1. What Is Harassment on NOIZ?

Harassment refers to any repeated, targeted, or malicious behavior designed to:

  • Threaten, intimidate, or humiliate others
  • Derail a stream, chat session, or community gathering
  • Leverage hate speech or personal insults to provoke or alienate
  • Pressure creators or viewers into discomfort, submission, or silence

This includes (but is not limited to):

  • Repeated trolling or flamebaiting across sessions
  • Following users across streams with antagonistic intent (stalking behavior)
  • Doxxing, impersonation, or threatening to release private info
  • Use of slurs, hate symbols, coded language, or discriminatory jokes
  • Chat spam targeting specific users with ridicule or commands

Harassment is evaluated based on behavior pattern, context, and impact—not just single incidents.

2. One-Off Joke vs. Harassment

It’s important to distinguish between an offhand comment and ongoing harassment. Here’s how NOIZ evaluates:

Behavior Example Likely Harassment?
“You’re dumb” in a heated moment❌ Probably not
“You’re dumb” every stream✅ Yes
“Rate me or else I’ll refund”✅ Yes
“I hate [group] people”✅ Yes (Hate speech)
“This is boring” once❌ No
“This stream sucks” every session✅ Yes

Intent + repetition = harassment, especially if aimed at identity, behavior, or expression.

3. How to Report Harassment

To report a message or user:

  1. Click the offending message or username
  2. Select “Report” from the dropdown
  3. Choose a reason:
    • Repeated or targeted behavior
    • Hate speech or discriminatory content
    • Threats, stalking, or impersonation
  4. Add a note or timestamp if context matters

All reports are first sent to the streamer’s mod team.
If flagged as serious or repeated, it is escalated to:

  • DCT (Dissonance Compliance Team)
  • Staff Admin (for legal or multi-stream cases)

You do not need to be the target to report — bystander reports are valid.

4. What Happens After a Report

For minor, first-time reports:

  • Platform may issue a warning, timeout, or chat mute
  • Mods may choose to use discretion if context seems accidental

For more serious or ongoing offenses:

  • Account may be flagged or suspended
  • Repeat or high-severity cases can lead to permanent bans
  • Reports involving doxxing, threats, or targeted harassment will be logged for legal escalation if necessary

You may not receive full visibility into the outcome due to privacy protections — but all valid reports are logged and tracked.

5. How NOIZ Defines Hate Speech

Hate speech includes any statement, symbol, or act that attacks or dehumanizes based on:

  • Race or ethnicity
  • Gender identity or expression
  • Sexual orientation
  • National origin or immigration status
  • Religion or belief
  • Age or disability

Prohibited content includes:

  • Slurs (including coded or “joke” variants)
  • Symbols tied to hate groups
  • Language that promotes supremacy, exclusion, or violence
  • Dog whistles or ironic bigotry (e.g., "just memes") used to provoke harm

There is zero tolerance for hate speech on NOIZ — regardless of intended humor or self-labeling as “free speech.”

6. What to Do If You’re Being Harassed

Immediate steps:

  • Use /block, /timeout, or /ban
  • Activate Mod-only chat or enable stricter auto-mod filters
  • If needed, flag your stream as "Safety Alert" in your Creator Dashboard

If off-platform or IRL harassment occurs:

  • See the Safety & Doxxing Policy for emergency protocols
  • Gather screenshots or chat logs if it’s safe to do so
  • Involve local authorities if there is real-world threat or stalking behavior

You don’t need to “tough it out.” NOIZ has your back — always.

7. False Reports and Abuse of the System

The integrity of the reporting system is essential. Submitting false reports to punish, troll, or silence others is a violation of our Community and Enforcement Policies.

Abuse may result in:

  • Temporary or permanent loss of reporting privileges
  • Monetization suspension
  • Account review or removal

Only report when necessary, and with good faith.

8. FAQ

Q: Will I be notified if someone is banned?
A: In most cases, no — but serious action will reflect in their account visibility or chat privileges.

Q: Can I delete a report I submitted?
A: No, but you can update your original note or submit clarifying information afterward.

Q: What if someone harasses me off-platform?
A: You may still report it if they are using NOIZ tools or connections to continue the behavior (e.g. streaming about you, referencing personal info, raiding with intent).

Q: Can I report a mod, DCT member, or NOIZ Staff?
A: Yes. Use the “Staff Conduct” category in the Report menu or email staffreview@noiz.gg. These are reviewed independently.
