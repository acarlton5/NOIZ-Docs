Content Moderation in the Marketplace

1. Why Moderation Matters

The NOIZ Marketplace exists to:

  • Empower creators
  • Provide streamers with high-quality overlays, emotes, and audio
  • Maintain a safe, legal, and inclusive environment

To do that, every submission must pass a content review. Moderation tools are in place to address:

  • Policy violations
  • Abuse or spam
  • Stolen assets
  • Unsafe or misleading listings

2. How Moderation Works

There are two layers of moderation:

Pre-publish Review

  • All new listings are manually reviewed before going live
  • Automated checks flag asset size, format, metadata, and tags
  • Human review verifies:
    • Originality
    • Quality
    • Policy compliance (visuals, text, audio)

Post-publish Reporting

  • Viewers, streamers, or other artists can flag listings
  • Reports are reviewed by Marketplace Moderators or escalated to Staff Admins
  • Repeat offenses can result in listing suspension, ⚡︎dB hold, or Marketplace removal

3. What Can Get Flagged or Removed

Violation TypeExample
❌ Stolen ContentReposted Twitch emotes, Canva templates, AI clones
❌ Offensive or NSFW AssetsPornographic overlays, gore emotes, hate symbols
❌ Deceptive Tags or Descriptions“Animated,” but static; “Partner badge,” but unofficial
❌ Malware or Script InjectionPlugin assets designed to mislead or exploit
❌ Spam ListingsDozens of minor variations with no functional change

Assets violating NOIZ’s rules may be:

  • Removed permanently
  • Temporarily suspended
  • Modified by staff (e.g., tag cleanup, forced privacy)

4. Artist Tools for Self-Moderation

Verified Artists can:

  • Edit live listings to fix issues
  • Disable visibility of outdated or broken assets
  • Lock asset previews or watermark them
  • Respond to moderation notices with explanation or dispute

⚠️ Ignoring repeated warnings may result in loss of Verified status.

5. Viewer Protections

If a user unlocks an asset and finds it:

  • Doesn’t work as described
  • Is clearly unsafe or misrepresented
  • Violates the NOIZ Code of Conduct

They can:

  • Report the listing via Inventory → Report
  • Leave a public review
  • Request a ⚡︎dB audit (if the unlock was part of a platform campaign)

6. Developer Plugin Asset Moderation

If you’re a developer:

  • Your plugin-linked assets (UI, animations, reactions) are subject to the same moderation rules
  • Visual abuse (e.g., flashing images with epileptic risk) will be flagged
  • Audio-triggered events must follow the Marketplace SFX guidelines
  • Plugins repeatedly flagged for abuse may be temporarily disabled platform-wide

7. FAQ

  • Can I appeal a listing removal? → Yes, through the support team or dashboard notification
  • Will I be told why a listing was removed? → Yes — a reason will always be provided
  • Can I resubmit a removed asset after fixing it? → Yes, after compliance
  • Can I moderate my own asset reviews? → You can respond, but not delete or hide ratings
