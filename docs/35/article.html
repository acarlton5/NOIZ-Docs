1. Why Regional Compliance Matters

NOIZ is a global platform — but streamers and viewers are still subject to local laws, regardless of platform features or moderation rules.

Depending on your country or region, the following may be restricted or regulated:

  • What can be said or shown during a broadcast
  • Which topics are considered politically or socially sensitive
  • How user data is collected, stored, or transmitted
  • Whether games or mechanics (like loot boxes or gacha pulls) are considered gambling
  • What age limits apply to creators or viewers

Streaming laws differ globally — and as a creator, you are responsible for following your region’s rules, even if they conflict with what’s allowed elsewhere on NOIZ.

2. General Platform Rule

NOIZ does not override your local laws.

If your stream violates laws in your country or region, you may be restricted, muted, or removed — even if the content is acceptable under NOIZ’s Terms of Service.

This applies to:

  • Game content and visual assets
  • On-stream behavior and speech
  • Plugin usage and data collection
  • Marketplace sales and token-related activity

4. Age Ratings & Maturity Controls

To help streamers remain compliant with local age-restriction rules, NOIZ supports:

  • Stream rating tags (Everyone, Teen, Mature, 18+)
  • Entry warnings for flagged streams
  • Auto-restriction by region if a game, tag, or plugin is not allowed in your location

You must accurately rate your own stream based on the content and the region you're streaming from.

In some countries, misrating a Mature or 18+ stream may result in a personal legal fine or criminal charge — not just platform action.

5. Plugins and Extensions

Plugins that interact with viewer data, create interactive overlays, or connect to third-party tools may have region-specific restrictions.

Examples:

  • Minors' data protection laws may ban plugins that collect usernames or age data
  • Data localization laws may require info to stay within national borders
  • Tracking or profiling tools may be prohibited under EU or South American laws

NOIZ will automatically geo-restrict plugins that fail compliance checks and label affected features in the Marketplace.

7. Gambling, Gacha, and Lootbox Content

Certain regions classify in-game mechanics like loot boxes, skin pulls, or spin-based gacha rolls as gambling. If your stream contains this:

✅ You must:

  • Use the “Game of Chance” stream tag
  • Rate your stream accordingly (e.g., Mature, 18+)
  • Comply with Quest restrictions for sponsored content

❌ You cannot:

  • Promote gambling mechanics to minors
  • Offer Decibel rewards for viewer bets or spins
  • Run untagged casino-style streams in restricted regions

Failure to follow this policy may result in region-specific muting or platform removal.

8. What Happens If You Break a Regional Law While Streaming?

If your stream violates local laws:

  • NOIZ may remove your content or limit its visibility in your country
  • Your VOD may be muted, clipped, or archived privately
  • Your channel may be flagged for legal risk or escalation

You may still be held personally accountable by your government, especially in regions where:

  • Game content is regulated (e.g. South Korea, Germany)
  • IRL streaming is considered surveillance (e.g. Japan)
  • Unlicensed monetization is penalized (e.g. some parts of the EU)

NOIZ cannot provide legal protection or override government action. You are streaming at your own legal risk.

9. FAQ

Can I stream an M-rated game in a country that bans it?
Not if your country prohibits it. You may be region-muted or account-flagged if detected.

What if a plugin I use isn’t allowed in my country?
It will be blocked automatically. You’ll see a warning in your dashboard.

Am I protected by NOIZ if local law disagrees with me?
No. NOIZ follows a compliance model — it cannot protect you from local authorities.

How do I restrict my stream visibility by region?
Go to: Settings → Stream Privacy → Regional Restrictions
Set visibility filters by country or language to comply with your local laws.
