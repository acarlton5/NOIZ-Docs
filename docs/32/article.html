Music Is the #1 DMCA Risk for Creators

Music is one of the most common causes of copyright enforcement on streaming platforms — and NOIZ is no exception. Even if you've purchased a song on Spotify, iTunes, or another service, you do not own the rights to publicly broadcast it on stream.

DMCA (Digital Millennium Copyright Act) applies to both live broadcasts and VOD replays. NOIZ honors valid takedown notices from rights holders, including music publishers, labels, and licensing platforms.

Just because you can listen to it doesn’t mean you can stream it.

What Music Is Safe to Use

You can safely use the following types of music in your stream:

  • Original music you composed and performed yourself
  • Licensed tracks that grant you the right to use them in public or streamed broadcasts
  • Royalty-free libraries with explicit permission for streaming (check the usage rights!)
  • NOIZ Marketplace music packs marked as “stream-safe” or “licensed for live broadcast”
  • Certain indie game soundtracks, as long as the developer grants permission (many do)

Look for clear license language like: “OK for streaming,” “Public broadcast allowed,” or “Live use permitted.”

What Music Is Not Safe to Use

These are not safe to stream:

  • Spotify, Apple Music, Amazon Music — these services are for personal use only
  • YouTube lyric videos, karaoke tracks, or remixes unless clearly marked as usable
  • Commercial music playing in your background (e.g., from a radio, car, or public space)
  • User-submitted tracks that aren’t verified with a proper license or explicit permission

Using these sources puts you at risk for takedowns, account flags, or muted VODs — even if you’re not the one playing the music directly.

How to Make Sure Your Music Is Stream-Safe

Always confirm the license type before using music on stream. You need the right to use it in live public broadcasts, not just private playback.

Trusted sources include:

  • StreamBeats
  • Pretzel Rocks (be sure to check the license filters)
  • Incompetech (many tracks are royalty-free with attribution)
  • NOIZ Marketplace — curated packs with explicit tags for stream use

If you're unsure whether the license applies to streaming, don’t use it.

Game Music and Licensed Titles

Many games include music that’s safe to use — but others include licensed tracks that can result in takedowns.

Game Type Safe? Notes
Indie games with original music ✅ Often allowed — check the developer’s terms or public FAQ
AAA titles (e.g. Nintendo) ❌ Risky — some publishers issue DMCA for the soundtrack alone
Licensed music inside games ❌ Always risky — disable “licensed music” in-game if possible (e.g. GTA)

When in doubt, mute the music channel of the game or toggle off licensed tracks in the settings.

What About Covers, Remixes, or AI-Generated Tracks?

Even if you perform or remix music yourself, the original composition still has copyright protection.

Covers still require:

  • Composition rights (typically obtained through a mechanical license)
  • Streaming rights for the performance (separate from the composition)

Remixes and AI-generated tracks must:

  • Be completely original, or
  • Use material from the public domain, or
  • Be properly licensed (with permission from all rights holders)

If your remix samples a copyrighted track — even briefly — it is not safe to use.

If You Get Flagged

If a takedown request is received:

  • You'll be notified privately via your NOIZ dashboard
  • The affected VOD or clip will be muted or removed
  • Repeat violations may limit access to:
    • VOD storage
    • Quest campaign approvals
    • Monetization tools

NOIZ will never disclose your identity to third-party requesters unless legally required.

FAQ

Can I sing copyrighted songs live?

No — unless you have explicit rights to the composition. Live covers are still subject to copyright law.

What if someone donates a sound with copyrighted music?

You are responsible for any content that plays on your stream, including alerts and user-submitted clips.

Can I play music only during breaks or intros?

Same rules apply. Music in intro screens still counts as broadcasted content.

How do I upload a music pack to the Marketplace?

Go to: Dashboard → Marketplace → New Listing → “Audio Pack”. You must include a license field confirming stream-safe usage.
