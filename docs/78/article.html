This policy ensures all Marketplace assets are properly credited, licensed, and rights-protected — safeguarding:

  • Artists from stolen/resold work
  • Buyers from unknowingly purchasing unlicensed assets
  • Streamers from TOS violations over asset use
  • NOIZ from legal disputes involving misrepresented content

1. Why This Matters

Accurate ownership and rights disclosure prevents:

  • Uncredited asset resale
  • Buyers losing access to art they thought they owned
  • Streamers being banned for using stolen content
  • NOIZ facing legal claims over improperly licensed Marketplace items

2. Who Owns What on NOIZ

By default:

  • The original creator of a listing holds the copyright
  • NOIZ receives a limited license to display/distribute it digitally
  • Buyers receive a non-transferable, personal-use license for NOIZ use only

Assets cannot be:

  • Resold
  • Sublicensed
  • Exported off-platform — unless the listing explicitly includes those rights.

3. Commissioned Work Requirements

If you upload work that was:

  • Commissioned by someone else
  • Created collaboratively by multiple people

You must:

  • Provide proof of rights (signed contract, agreement, or explicit license)
  • Disclose in the listing that it’s collaborative or licensed work
  • Use accurate credits (NOIZ supports co-listing & attribution tags)

Failure to comply may result in:

  • Listing removal
  • ⚡︎dB earnings freeze
  • Marketplace access suspension

4. Collaborative Projects

You may publish collaborative assets if:

  • All contributors are credited
  • One uploader is designated as payout owner
  • Co-creators are tagged in the listing (visible or silent credit)

Shared revenue features are planned, but not yet available — payouts currently go to the uploader’s account.

5. Accepting Commissions via NOIZ

Verified Artists can:

  • Accept ⚡︎dB commissions (manual or Marketplace listing)
  • Enable “Commission Available” toggle on their storefront
  • Mark listings as “Commissioned By” with viewer-submitted credit

Commission listings must include:

  • Rights granted (e.g., “NOIZ use only” vs. “full rights granted”)
  • Public or private listing status (private = not searchable)
  • Optional watermark or client alias in previews

6. What’s Not Allowed

❌ Strictly prohibited:

  • Uploading work you didn’t create or license
  • Listing Fiverr/AI-generated art without credit and licensing rights
  • Using false credits to obtain Verified Artist status
  • Mass-reposting Twitch or other platform assets without adapting to NOIZ licensing

7. FAQ

Can I sell something I made for Twitch?
Yes, if you fully own the rights and relicense it for NOIZ.
Can I post fanart of a copyrighted character?
No — fanart of copyrighted IP cannot be listed in the Marketplace.
Can I credit someone who helped but doesn’t want payout?
Yes — add acknowledgment in the description.
Can buyers use my art off-platform?
Only if the license explicitly allows it.
