What Is NOIZ as a Platform?

NOIZ is a creator-first live streaming ecosystem that reimagines how digital support and infrastructure work together.

Unlike traditional platforms that prioritize monetization through fiat-based transactions or advertising, NOIZ centers its economy around Decibels — a virtual support token system. This framing allows NOIZ to foster community-driven growth, non-exploitative monetization, and meaningful interaction across the platform.

Rather than relying on centralized server farms, NOIZ is powered by a decentralized relay infrastructure, hosted by users who contribute bandwidth and routing (Pathweavers). The entire system is designed to be lightweight, scalable, and fair — without relying on crypto, ads, or pay-to-win mechanics.

Channels: Your Creator Hub

Each verified user on NOIZ has a channel — their home base for broadcasting, engaging with viewers, and customizing their community experience.

A NOIZ channel includes:

  • Your live video stream (with viewer-adjustable quality and latency settings)
  • A custom channel banner, bio, and linked socials
  • A fully-featured chat system with moderation controls and bot plugin integration
  • The ability to set up Reverb (channel points), interactive Quests, and visual badges
  • Channel-specific boosts, overlays, and command shortcuts

Viewers passively earn Reverb just by being present and interacting, which can then be spent on channel-specific redemptions, visual effects, or boosts.

Channels evolve as creators grow — additional tools and cosmetic unlocks are added automatically through progression.

Streams: How Broadcasting Works on NOIZ

NOIZ supports streaming via two methods:

  • NOIZ Studio, a customized OBS fork tailored for platform-native integration
  • Standard OBS with a modified configuration, supporting the ITGl routing system

Low-latency streaming is the default behavior on NOIZ. This enables highly responsive chat interactions and better integration with time-sensitive features like Quests and Boosts.

Additional stream capabilities include:

  • Embedded streams on external sites
  • Boostable visibility via Reverb or Decibel contributions
  • Raiding other creators post-stream
  • Eligibility for Echostage, NOIZ’s dynamic spotlight algorithm

Unlike platforms with fixed bitrate tiers, NOIZ dynamically adjusts transcodes and quality levels based on viewer activity and infrastructure availability — ensuring consistent performance without punishing creators for lower viewership.

Quests: Dynamic Viewer Interaction

Quests are a core part of NOIZ’s interactive ecosystem.

They are goal-based challenges that encourage viewers to engage in meaningful ways, such as:

  • Watching for a certain amount of time
  • Sending chat messages
  • Triggering overlays or boosts
  • Gifting subs or completing event actions

Upon completion, viewers may receive:

  • dB (faded Decibels)
  • Cosmetic badges or in-stream visuals
  • Progress toward long-term milestones

Some quests are created by creators. Others are sponsored — meaning they're funded by games, brands, or NOIZ itself.

Importantly, all quest mechanics are action-based and cannot be bought or skipped — preserving a fair, community-first experience.

Relays: The Backbone of NOIZ Infrastructure

Unlike traditional centralized streaming platforms, NOIZ runs on a relay node network hosted by trusted members of the community — called Pathweavers.

Relays are:

  • Lightweight servers that forward stream data to viewers
  • Selected automatically based on trust, latency, and uptime
  • Never responsible for moderation or content — only delivery

Relay operators can earn ⚡︎dB (charged Decibels) as a recognition of their contribution to infrastructure uptime and load balancing.

Relay performance is monitored internally by the platform, using metrics like:

  • Packet integrity
  • Latency stability
  • Route reliability

There is no crypto mining, no proof-of-work, and no speculative earning — only platform-controlled recognition for valuable backend participation.

How Everything Connects

At NOIZ, the ecosystem is tightly interwoven. Here’s how each system fits together:

  • Channels are where creators manage their stream, community, and engagement tools.
  • Streams deliver the content — handled by optimized software and routed through trusted relays.
  • Quests turn passive viewers into active participants by rewarding them for supporting and engaging with the stream.
  • Relays distribute the stream with low latency and resilience, ensuring smooth playback even during peak load.

All of this is powered by Decibels, which come in two forms:

  • dB — unlocked by viewers for use on the platform (not eligible for redemption)
  • ⚡︎dB — earned by creators through support and eligible for redemption (if monetization requirements are met)

Every action — whether you're watching, boosting, hosting a relay, or broadcasting — feeds into this system, ensuring that everyone’s contribution is recognized.
